Concept 1: the unspeakable adjective

To work with topics around dark emotions, I think it‚Äôs important to balance what people feel strongly inside and what people are willing to share with others. No matter it is jealousy, suspicion, or the desire to control, we tend to hide these feelings as it‚Äôs not comfortable to say them out aloud frankly whether it‚Äôs to yourself or to others.
What you say is not always what you feel, and sometimes there are two sides about the same feeling. For example, when you feel jealous towards someone, you might not say frankly that you‚Äôre jealous, instead, you might say that you admire the person, or you might not even admit that you‚Äôre jealous. It is interesting to think about how these emotions are expressed differently to others and to yourself. Outwards emotions tend to be a social function, as we use it to communicate. A lot of the time what we express to others might not be our true feelings, we do it for the sake of communication. We feel, we find a way that is accepted by the public opinion and then we express it. But for inwards emotions, can you be honesty to yourself? Have you noticed how many steps you have taken before you finally give definition to ‚Äúhow do you feel now‚Äù?
So, I think it might be a promising direction to focus on the two sides of emotions, and explore more about how people lie about their feelings, no matter in purpose or unintentional.
