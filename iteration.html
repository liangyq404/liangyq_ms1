2020.11.20 - 12.13

Iteration 1.0

Feedback from 1 on 1 meeting with Professor Fan
  • The most important thing of modern art is the process rather than the final result. Focusing more on the peeling behavior itself could bring emotions to the audience. Use the engaging process to enrich the audience’s experience. The whole process could be the biggest meaning of the project.
  • Start with specific topics instead of abstract emotions.
  • The peeling process could also be a kind of interface that goes from outside to inside.
  • The trigger to the interaction is important
  • What is the core? It could be what you didn’t expect from the outside, or it could be nothing at all.
  • How to build a seductive interface and attract viewers to interact
  • Art doesn’t need to be educational.
  • Peeling could also be a form of timeline.
  • Select and reform media that people are familiar with.
  • Food design. To peel a virtual space.
  • Use transparency to represent the multi-layers constructer
  • Scent that transforms with heat.
  • What are the structures that could be peeled? To observe life more carefully
  • Synthesis of process materials.
  • Art Archaeology, to discover within the process of exploring.
  • How to build the sequence & system.
  • It could also be a way of narrative, and it changes when the audience gets involved.
Iteration 1.0

Something I wish to stick to:

  • The purpose to show the assistance of the social pressure on hidden emotions
  • The object has some hints that attract the audience’s attention and Like exposing part of the inner layer to suggest that it can be peeled.
Something I wish to change:
  • Instead of creating a small multi-layer object that only allows one audience to hold it and peel it, why not create a large one that hangs in the middle of an exhibition room and allows multi audience to interact with it at the same time, while each audience can have a unique result of how much they wanna peel it and how many layers they wanna explore.
  • Use stethoscopes to amplify the peeling sound. Enriching the multi-sensory immersive experience. The audience can hear the sound of the material when they do it. It could be a fake stethoscope that connects to the pre-edited sound file.
  • Emotion is not what I would put forward directly at the beginning of the experience, but let the audience to feel it in the process of peeling, with the trigger of luring them to continue and the auditory feedback.
References:
  • LARYNX: an interactive installation that visualizes sound
  • Cut Piece - Yoko Ono
Expected Outcome
  • An irregularly shaped object hanging in the exhibition room, with multiple layers on the inside and the core would be a form related to naked body. So when you peel the layers off, you gradually see parts of the body coming out.
  • A video/projection showing from the object’s perspective, that it’s being peeled off. This is to represent the object taking the initiative to seduce the audience to peel it and expose what’s hidden inside.

2020.12.7

Iteration 2.0
Cut Piece is an art performance by Yoko Ono. It was performed several times under different social statuses and in different location. During the performance, the artist sits on the ground, and each audience could come to the stage and cut one piece of her clothes to take away, this goes on until the artist became totally naked. I'm greatly inspired by the brave invitation that the artist gave to the audience to cut her clothes off, as well as the subtle emotion evoked during the whole process.
It comes to me that, the active self-exposing could be a reverse way to show resistance to the environment. If the object successfully seduced the audience to peel it's outer skin off, it could be a successful protest to the social pressure. Here, the inner layer of the object is a metaphor for the social pressure that pushes you to hide your unspeakable emotions. While peeling off the layers until you see the core, you are invited to engage in the protest, and in the end, you will find the objects staring straight at you (by finding the camera towards the audience inside the layers), showing the repressive power of questioning.
