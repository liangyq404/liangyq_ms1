Day 1 | Regret

Guiding questions
  1. What are things that you regret you have done & haven't done?
  2. Why you did/didn't do it?
  3. How to materialize your regrets?
Inspiration

Regret is one of the most powerful emotions that I could think of. There is a common attitude towards regret that it would be a waste of time to think about these things afterwards, cause there’s nothing you can do to change what happened in the past. However, the more you hide it, the deeper it influences you. Spitting it out could actually help you to face the lost chances and gather more courage for the future.
I wonder what kind of things people would feel regret about, and what kind of stories these emotions are linked to. Time, location, counterparts, and the relationship itself, all could be cause one’s regret from many dimensions.

Reflection

While doing the research, a lot of people say that they have nothing to regret about, which is a really positive attitude towards life.
According to Brain Wise, “The more opportunity you have, the more regret you'll feel”. So another positive thing about regret is that you’re having more opportunities to make your own decision and learn from it.
Which surprised me is that some people started to feel depressed after thinking about the question thoroughly, showing the great impact that regret can have on people.
